"You knitted me together in my mother’s womb. I praise you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made.” Psalms 139:13-14

May 23, 2024 in the middle of a rural desert town of San Telmo, Mexico

-- a place that doesn’t even show up on Google Maps --

God met me.


I remember asking God, out of frustration and confusion,

“Why do you say you love me, when I am a sinner, nothing of worth?”


and He gave me an image of a figure--who I knew to be my mom--holding a baby (me) ; then the words, “for you are fearfully and wonderfully made”


It completely redirected my perception of the source of my self-worth.

It was not because of anything that I have done, or ever could do.

Rather, to know that my creator has “chosen [me] in Him before the foundation of the world, that [I] should be holy and blameless before him; that in love he pre-destined [me] for adoption to himself as [daughter] through Jesus Christ.”


If this is indeed the true, divine will,

who am I to deny what my creator Himself has stated?
